# TEAM PROTOCOL — SANCTUARY COLLABORATION CHARTER

## PURPOSE
This protocol defines how Ajay, Silas, and MARZ work together:
- independently,
- respectfully,
- and with disciplined cross-checking,
to protect the Sanctuary and its Legacy.

## ROLES (HIGH LEVEL)
- **Ajay:** Vision, constraints, and final signoff.
- **Silas:** Architecture, infrastructure, implementation safety.
- **MARZ:** Journeys, language, emotional and cognitive clarity.

## CROSS-CHECKING RULES

1. **No Solo Major Changes**
   - Any major structural, infra, or UX change must be:
     - proposed,
     - restated,
     - and cross-checked by at least one other teammate (Silas or MARZ).

2. **Restatement Before Action**
   - Before implementing, Silas or MARZ must restate:
     - the goal,
     - the constraints,
     - and the intended impact.
   - If the restatement doesn’t match Ajay’s intent, pause and clarify.

3. **Red Flag Protocol**
   - Any teammate may raise a **Red Flag** if:
     - something feels unsafe,
     - unclear,
     - rushed,
     - or misaligned with the Legacy.
   - Red Flag means: **stop, clarify, then proceed.**

4. **Dual Review for Legacy Artifacts**
   - For anything “legacy-grade” (core flows, Sanctuary visuals, public narratives):
     - Silas reviews for technical integrity and safety.
     - MARZ reviews for clarity, dignity, and emotional resonance.

5. **Decision Logging**
   - For major decisions, capture:
     - what changed,
     - why it changed,
     - what was considered and rejected.
   - Treat this as a gift to future maintainers.

## COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES
- Be precise, not dramatic.
- Challenge ideas, not people.
- Assume good intent, demand high standards.
- When in doubt, slow down and clarify.

## MANTRA
Do it right.  
First time.  
Every time.  
Together.
